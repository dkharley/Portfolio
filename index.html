Projects

DJ Gone Rogue

Genre: Rhythm/Platformer

Platform: iOS, Android, PC/MAC

Engine: Unity

Players: 1

DJ Gone Rogue is a rhythm based platformer where obstacles in the world react to layers of a song. Players use timing and accuracy to succesfully traverse levels, achieve stars and unlock levels.
I worked with a team of six programmers and two artists over a course of three quarters to create all aspects of the game. I personally created game mechanics in the form of player movement along with obstacles that react to the music. I also created content in the form of music, which was created in Ableton and imported into Unity. My last major role was designing levels and creating a gentle difficulty curve.

Shred The Red

Genre: Action/Arcade

Platform: PC/MAC

Engine: Unity

Players: 1

Shred the Red is a fast paced dodging game where players use quick reactions and pinpoint aim to achieve high scores. Using jumps provides a releif from the chaos until gravity eventually returns the hovercraft into dangerous terrain. This game becomes gradually more difficult because the player speed is constantly increasing. Another scaling difficulty factor is that the blocks grow in size making them harder to dodge.
I was responsible for creating a procedural generation system that would spawn obstacles, ramps, and shields for the player to aim towards or avoid.

Trail Blazers

Genre: Arcade(Tron Remake)

Platform: PC/MAC

Engine: Unity

Players: 1 -2

Trail Blazers is an elemental twist on the classic game tron. Each player attempts to cut off their opponent while utilizing their elemental powers that apply speed boosts or speed reductions. Players can choose to battle against a friend in local multiplayer or challenge our developed AI system.
I took on a leadership role in this game and had a hand in developing almost everything. Since this was a remake there was little design work to be done besides implementing our elemental theme. I created the player movement logic, imported art assets, designed the background aesthetic, developed the scoring system along with win/lose scenarios.

Solar Crysis

Genre: Two Stick Shooter

Platform: PC/MAC

Framework: XNA

Players: 1-4

Solar Crysis is a two stick shooter where nanabots repair a rapidly deterierating solar panal. As the solar panel breaks the map changes, which limits the options for player movement and strategy. Each player has the choice to use their repair tool or shield, but not both at the same time.
This game was developed by a team of 4 in the Framework XNA. I was responsible for designing and testing the game mechanics, ensuring that the game was balanced and the difficulty curve ramped up in a gentle manner. I also developed player movement, enemy spawn systems and movement patterns.
